instructions

  1. Wash the rice: Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear to remove excess starch.
  2. Soak the rice: Soak the washed rice in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside
  3. Marinate the meat: If using chicken, mutton, beef, fish, shrimp, or vegetables, marinate with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, salt, and biryani spices (like turmeric, red chili powder, cumin, coriander powder) for at least 30 minutes to overnight in the refrigerator.
  4. Fry onions: Heat ghee or oil in a large pot. Fry sliced onions until golden brown and crispy. Remove and set aside for garnishing.
  5. Cook the meat (if using): In the same pot, fry ginger-garlic paste until fragrant. Add marinated meat and cook until it changes color and is partially cooked.

  7. Dissolve saffron: Dissolve saffron strands in warm milk and drizzle over the top layer of ric
  8. Sealing the pot: Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal with aluminum foil to trap steam.
  9. Cook on low heat: Cook on low heat (dum) for about 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the rice to cook through.
  10. After 20-25 minutes, check if the rice is cooked and the meat (if using) is tender. If not, cover and cook for a few more minutes.
  11. Garnish with fried onions, fresh cilantro, and mint leaves. Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) or salan (spicy curry).
